What is Neutering?
  • Neutering

    • Neutering is a surgical procedure performed on animals, mainly cats and dogs. It involves the removal of reproductive organs: testicles in males (castration) and ovaries/uterus in females (spaying). Neutering is done under general anesthesia. In males, neutering removes the source of sperm and male hormones like testosterone. In females, spaying prevents heat cycles, eliminates the possibility of pregnancy, and reduces the chances of reproductive disorders and cancers. Neutering is a routine and safe procedure commonly performed by veterinary professionals.
